Workflow examples

What AI can usefully automate

The useful workflows are usually narrow, measurable and close to a real operational bottleneck. The aim is not to replace the team. The aim is to collect, classify, summarise, remind and route.

Missed-call recovery

Send a fast approved SMS after a missed call, capture intent, identify urgency and hand over a clean summary.

Website lead capture

Turn page visits into structured enquiries using short forms, readiness scores, calculators or guided questions.

Email response drafting

Read an inbound message, identify the next step, draft a reply and flag anything that needs human review.

Follow-up reminders

Follow up quotes, proposals, consultations, appointments or inactive conversations without relying on memory.

Handover summaries

Turn messy calls, chats and emails into structured notes with intent, urgency, next action and escalation flags.

AI SEO support

Use AI to turn real expertise into useful landing pages, FAQs, lead magnets and follow-up sequences.

Implementation field note: The first useful workflow is rarely the broadest one. It is usually the workflow that is repetitive, measurable and already costing the team time or revenue.

Method

How we map the first workflow

Before recommending tools, we map the operational problem. This keeps the project narrow, measurable and useful to the team.

1. Find the leakage point

Missed calls, slow replies, abandoned forms, inconsistent follow-up, manual chasing or admin that depends on memory.

2. Define the safe scope

What AI can collect, classify, draft, summarise or route — and what must always go to a human.

3. Design the handover

The exact summary the team needs: customer type, intent, urgency, next action and escalation flags.

4. Estimate the value

We model potential value using enquiry volume, response speed, conversion assumptions and staff time.

Why not just install a chatbot?

A chatbot may be useful, but it is rarely the best first step. Many businesses get more value from missed-call recovery, lead capture, enquiry triage, follow-up workflows and handover summaries.

These workflows are easier to control, easier to measure and closer to real operational problems.

Better first workflows

  • Missed-call recovery
  • Website lead capture
  • New enquiry triage
  • Quote follow-up
  • Handover summaries

Riskier first workflows

  • Broad AI chat
  • Advice-heavy answers
  • Complaint handling without review
  • Fully automated decisions
  • Uncontrolled booking changes

Where AI automation fails

AI projects usually fail for ordinary operational reasons, not because the model is not clever enough.

  • The workflow is too broad.
  • Nobody owns the handover.
  • The team receives too many alerts.
  • The tone feels pushy or unnatural.
  • No one checks early outputs.
  • There is no success metric.

The lesson is simple: a better model rarely fixes a poorly owned workflow. The handover, escalation rules, feedback loop and measurement plan matter more.
