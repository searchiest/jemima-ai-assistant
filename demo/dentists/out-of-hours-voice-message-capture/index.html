What this demo does

This dental voice demo captures routine out-of-hours or reception-overflow enquiries without answering factual dental questions, booking a real appointment, or giving clinical advice.

What happens next

The caller speaks naturally. Jemima captures the reason for the call, repeats the message back, stores it, and creates a structured call report showing caller intent, call reason, urgency flag, recommended staff action, confidence and whether human review is required.

What this demonstrates

Dental voice enquiry capture, reception overflow handling, out-of-hours message capture, natural spoken input, caller message confirmation, structured call reporting, staff action recommendations and safe human-review handover.

Business outcome

Replace dental voicemail with a structured call report that tells the practice what the caller wanted and what the team should do next.

Example voice conversation

Caller enters demo code: 2323

Jemima says: Hi, this is Jemima. In this demo, I’m working as an AI phone assistant for Smile Dental Practice. The team is unavailable right now, so please briefly tell me what you’re calling about, and I’ll show how the message can be captured for the team.

Caller says: I want to know if you accept NHS patients.

Jemima replies: Thanks. In this demo, I’ve captured that you’d like to know whether the practice accepts NHS patients. The message has been stored, and a call report is now available for the team to review and act on. Goodbye.

Demo boundaries

This is a demo. No real appointment is booked. No factual business answer is guaranteed. No medical, dental, legal, financial, technical, safety, diagnosis, treatment, pricing, availability or emergency advice is provided. Routine enquiries are captured for the team to review.

Why this workflow matters

This voice demo shows how a dental practice can capture missed-call and out-of-hours enquiries without relying only on voicemail. The caller speaks naturally, Jemima repeats the message back, and the practice receives a structured call report.

  • Reception may be busy with patients and unable to answer every call.
  • Out-of-hours callers may leave vague voicemails that take time to review.
  • Routine questions such as NHS availability, opening times, booking requests or callback requests need a clear handover.
  • The practice needs message capture without unsupported factual answers or clinical advice.
  • Staff need caller intent, urgency flag and recommended next action rather than just an audio recording.

What the business receives

The workflow turns an unanswered dental phone call into a structured business handover. It helps the practice team see what the caller wanted, whether a callback is needed, whether the message sounds urgent, and what action reception should take next.
