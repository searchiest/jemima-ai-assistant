Where time goes

Where accountancy firms lose time

Chasing records at deadline time

VAT, year-end and self-assessment all need documents from clients who go quiet. Manual chasing is repetitive and easy to drop.

Slow follow-up on enquiries

New-client and quote enquiries arrive mixed and unstructured. A slow reply loses good monthly clients before onboarding starts.

Onboarding new companies

New limited company owners ask about bookkeeping, corporation tax, VAT, payroll and director salary all at once — and need ordering.

Repetitive client reminders

Bookkeeping nudges, VAT deadlines and document requests are sent by hand, again and again, every cycle.

Out-of-hours and overflow calls

Calls during busy periods or after hours go to voicemail, then someone has to listen back and create a task.

Triaging mixed enquiries

Spotting which enquiries are recurring-fee opportunities vs one-off questions takes partner time that could be billable.

FAQ

Questions accountancy firms ask

Is client data kept secure and confidential?

Workflows are built with controlled access, secure document links and clear limits, and sensitive cases can stay under human review. We design around your confidentiality and GDPR obligations.

Does it work with Xero and QuickBooks?

We aim to fit around your existing software where possible, including Xero, QuickBooks, FreeAgent and Sage, plus your inboxes, sheets and practice tools.

Will it give clients tax advice?

No. Jemima captures, organises and follows up — the accountant always provides the actual advice and approvals.

Does it handle MTD and deadline cycles?

Reminder and document-chasing workflows can be scheduled around VAT, MTD, year-end and self-assessment cycles.

How long until something is live?

Simple workflows can be built quickly; we start with a practical review, then build and test in stages.

Do we stay in control?

Always. We build in approvals, logs and human handover points so your team oversees what the workflow does.
