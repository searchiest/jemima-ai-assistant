What this demonstrates

SMS appointment confirmation, change-request capture, human-review handover and legal-sector safety boundaries.

What happens next

The workflow sends a consultation message, captures the client response, asks for a preferred time if needed and updates the session.

Demo boundaries

This is a demo. No real appointment is changed. No legal advice is provided. Do not send confidential case information by SMS.

Why this SMS workflow matters

This SMS demo shows how a law firm can confirm or rearrange consultation attendance without asking the client to explain their legal matter by text. It is designed for operational appointment handling only.

  • Missed consultations waste fee-earner and admin time.
  • Clients may need to confirm or rearrange without phoning reception.
  • Legal teams need a clear appointment action, not confidential case details by SMS.
  • Change requests should be reviewed by staff before a real diary update.
  • SMS wording must avoid legal advice and confidential information collection.

Example SMS conversation

Client texts: 2621
Jemima replies: Your consultation is currently listed for Wednesday at 2:00pm. Reply CONFIRM to confirm, CHANGE to request another time, or DECLINE if you can no longer attend.
Client replies: CHANGE
Jemima replies: Please reply with your preferred day or time. Please do not send confidential case details by SMS.
Client replies: Friday morning
Jemima replies: Thanks. Your request for Friday morning has been captured. The firm would check availability before making any change.

What the business receives

The workflow helps the firm protect consultation time while maintaining clear boundaries around legal advice and confidentiality. Admin receives a clean scheduling action without asking the client to describe their matter by SMS.
